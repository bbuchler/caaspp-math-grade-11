# Course Brief

## Course

- Title: CAASPP Mathematics Success Academy - Grade 11
- Pilot: Week 1 - Linear Equations, Inequalities, and Functions
- Audience: 11th grade students preparing for CAASPP mathematics
- Format: Self-guided, click-through, mobile-friendly course
- Timebox: 4 weeks total; Week 1 pilot has five lessons

## Purpose

Students practice the math habits CAASPP rewards: solving accurately, choosing useful representations, explaining reasoning, reading graphs/tables, and applying math to realistic situations.

The course should feel like test prep without feeling like punishment. Lessons should be short, direct, visually supported, and built around problems students can imagine: rideshare costs, event budgets, phone plans, school fundraisers, and data from everyday decisions.

## Source Base

- Teacher-provided course overview and Week 1 workbook
- Official CAASPP and Smarter Balanced public resources
- Khan Academy videos used as optional instructional support
- Teacher-created practice items aligned to the Week 1 objectives

## Course Claims Emphasized

- Claim 1: Concepts and Procedures
- Claim 2: Problem Solving
- Claim 3: Communicating Reasoning
- Claim 4: Modeling and Data Analysis

## Week 1 Learning Targets

Students will be able to:

- solve one-step and two-step linear equations
- solve and graph simple inequalities
- connect equations, tables, graphs, and verbal situations
- interpret rate of change, intercepts, and function notation
- explain reasoning in short written answers
- use feedback to revise a math explanation

## Student Experience Requirements

- Each lesson has one video or visual support with contextual questions below it.
- Each lesson includes a worked example, guided practice, independent practice, and exit check.
- Students get immediate feedback on objective items.
- Short explanations are graded by rubric and flagged for teacher review when uncertain.
- Students who miss the exit check get targeted review instead of vague "try again" feedback.

## Privacy

Do not include learner identifiers, rosters, grades, protected support records, behavior notes, or identifiable learner work in prompts or course files.
