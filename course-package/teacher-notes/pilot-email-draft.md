# CAASPP Math Pilot Email Draft

Subject: Quick look at the CAASPP Math Lesson 1 pilot

Hi [Teacher Name],

I’m putting together a short CAASPP Math prep course for 11th grade, and I’d love your feedback on the first fully built lesson before we build out the rest of the course.

Right now, Lesson 1 is called **Undo the Equation**. The goal is for students to understand that solving an equation means undoing one step at a time while keeping both sides balanced. It is meant for students who need direct, scaffolded practice with the kind of equation-solving thinking that shows up inside CAASPP word problems, tables, graphs, and function questions.

Here is how the lesson is structured:

- **Overview video:** gives students the big idea before they start calculating.
- **Overview reflection:** asks students to explain the main idea in their own words.
- **Lesson vocabulary:** defines key terms like coefficient, equation, inverse operation, and solution.
- **Quick start:** a simple conceptual warm-up so students begin with the main idea, not a full word problem.
- **Video questions:** students answer questions directly under the video as they watch.
- **Worked examples and visuals:** show how to undo operations and check a solution.
- **Hands-on activity:** lets students interact with the idea of moving through equation-solving steps.
- **Real-world problem:** uses a club merch/ticket-style situation so students connect the equation to an actual context.
- **Retryable practice:** students can keep trying these questions until the concept starts to click.
- **Exit check:** this is the final quiz-style evidence for the lesson.
- **Finish line:** students see what they should now understand, can try an optional challenge, and submit the lesson.

The grading idea is that the course should support learning as students work. Practice is retryable. The exit check is not automatically retryable because it gives us a cleaner picture of whether the student can do the skill independently. Objective math answers are auto-graded, and written explanations can be graded with AI feedback using a rubric. Teachers can still override grades or regrade individual responses.

The teacher dashboard is being built to show the gradebook, student work samples, password resets, and review flags. If a student does not pass the exit check, the dashboard should flag that student for review/remediation. Once more students use the course, we can use the same saved question data to show trends, like which questions students are missing, what concepts need reteaching, and possible small-group review suggestions.

What I’m hoping you can look for:

- Does the lesson sequence make sense for students who need CAASPP support?
- Are the sections clear enough without too much explanation?
- Is the amount of scaffolding right?
- Does the exit check feel like fair evidence that they understood the lesson?
- If you like the Lesson 1 structure, would this be a good pattern for the other 19 lessons?

Thanks for taking a look. I’m trying to get Lesson 1 right first, then use that structure to build the rest of the course.
