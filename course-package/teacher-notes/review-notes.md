# Teacher Review Notes

## What Changed From The Original Week 1 Workbook

The original Week 1 workbook repeated essentially the same one-step equation structure for all five days. This pilot keeps the useful "watch, practice, selected response, constructed response, exit ticket" rhythm but turns the week into a progression:

1. equations
2. inequalities
3. linear representations
4. slope/intercepts in context
5. function notation and mini performance task

## Tone Goal

Keep the course direct and friendly. Students should feel like the lesson is saying:

"Here is the move. Here is why it works. Try it. Now use it in a situation that could actually show up on a test."

Avoid making the course feel like a packet copied into a screen.

## Engagement Notes

- Use familiar contexts: rideshare, lunch budget, gym membership, school dance, spirit shirts.
- Keep videos short and purposeful.
- Let students answer small questions while watching, not after a long video.
- Use infographics as job aids students can refer back to.
- Let Study Buddy generate similar practice, but keep graded answers protected.

## Math Implementation Notes

- Numeric and multiple-choice items can be auto-graded by code.
- Constructed explanations should use the rubrics in `assessments/open-response-rubrics.md`.
- Function notation should be taught as input/output first, notation second.
- Performance tasks should reward practical interpretation, not just calculation.
